Wolf Corridor in Yellowstone National Park

View Map
ArcGIS Pro

The map displays the optimal route for gray wolves in Yellowstone National Park, generated using Least Cost Path Analysis. The cost surfaces were generated based on the predefined weighted criteria such as land cover and distance to human. Cost distance and cost direction were generated to identify the least-cost path from the predefined origin to the predefined destination. The accumulated cost surface indicates that the majority of Yellowstone National Park constitutes suitable habitat for gray wolves. The optimal wolf corridor is 162.21 km long, running from Mammoth in the north to Badger Creek in the south.

Earthquake Risk Assessment of Yokohama, Japan

View Map
ArcGIS Pro

The map displays low-risk and high-risk areas in Yokohama, Japan, in the event of a catastrophic earthquake. Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) was conducted using predefined criteria such as population density and soil type. Risk surfaces were generated for each criterion and the corresponding weightings were determined using the Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP). The final risk surface was generated through the Weighted Overlay tool in ArcGIS Pro. Based on the results, areas of relatively high risk are concentrated in the eastern part of Yokohama.

Exploring Interpolation with the Spatial Analyst Extension in ArcGIS Pro

View Report
ArcGIS Pro Microsoft Word

The report compares different spatial interpolation methods that are available in ArcGIS Pro. DEMs (digital elevation models) were generated for each spatial interpolation method (Inverse Distance Weighting, Spline, Topo to Raster, Natural Neighbor). Based on the results, all spatial interpolation methods estimate the unknown values fairly accurately. The Topo to Raster interpolation had the best performance in estimating unknown values based on collected sample data. However, the Natural Neighbor interpolation was the most efficient method for users who do not have much knowledge about interpolation and want to generate interpolated surfaces quickly. Each interpolation method has their own advantages and disadvantages, and users should choose an interpolation method based on their research purposes and context.

Ski Resort Site Suitability Analysis

View Map
ArcGIS Pro

The map shows the optimal ski resort locations in Peterborough, Ontario. The interpolated DEM was used to derive the slope and aspect of the study area. The study area was then classified as "suitable" or "not suitable" using the Raster Calculator and Reclassify tools in ArcGIS Pro. Based on the results, the area of optimal ski resort locations for Green Circle (beginner) is 17.44 km2, whereas the area for Blue Square (intermediate) is 0.02 km2.
